Inspired by work by Rick Raddatz (QBasic Nibbles), Iulian-Gabriel Radu, Sean MacIsaac, Ian Peters, Guillaume Béland and probably others.


One of my first encounters with programming was back in the day on a 80286 with MS-DOS. It came with QBasic and two games, one was called Nibbles.


First I changed the Player Names and took a copy to school to play against my classmates.

Later we could start at any level and some versions gave certain players invinsibility.

Of course we needed per player choice of input mode. Some liked 2-button others prefered the 4-button mode.

Motivated by the short breaks we had to play, we tended to make the game more difficult than easier.

Making new levels was hard work, but we did make some.


I say 'we' though I was responsible for 99% of the coding, I valued my friends input just as much.


In a moment of nostalgia, I searched for Linux-based versions of Nibbles and stumbled upon GNOME Nibbles.

After having played a couple rounds I yearned for the options to start at any level and mainly build my own levels.



So over the Holidays I began a level designer and soon started coding game logic. After having had reasonable AI, I browsed the GNOME Nibbles project source and adapted it's AI.

Adding more AI players was just done because it's now easily possible on todays hard-ware.


I've adapted the major aspects of the game's behavior to that of GNOME Nibbles. Points, speed etc. should feel similar on same hard-ware.


The icons are almost all based on those in GNOME Nibbles project.

Audio snipets have been copied with only 2 changes:

1. I had to convert them to .wav as QSoundEffect did not play .ogg files

2. The teleporter sound was too aggressive for my taste, so I made it less so.


What does SwissalpS Nibblers add to GNOME Nibbles?

  • Level designer
  • More levels possible
  • More AI-worms
  • More health possible
  • Start at any Level
  • Two-button steering
  • Mouse-button steering
  • Setting for Game-Over only when all worms have run out of health
  • On every level spawn-points are distributed randomly among worms
  • Name worms before game-end
  • High score is not defined as there are so many configurables that I resigned to making a sortable table with many columns
  • Three modes on encounter of an unplayable level
  • AI-worms hug walls less closely
